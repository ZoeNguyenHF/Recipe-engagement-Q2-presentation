Feature name
Community Rating
Hypothesis
If we display community ratings prominently throughout the platform, then users will be motivated to rate recipes because they see their contributions directly influence the community and help others make decisions. This creates a feedback loop: visible ratings drive more rating engagement while providing social proof that supports confident meal selection.
View in prototype
Browse recipe cards throughout the interface to see star ratings and review counts displayed on each card. Click into recipe details to view expanded rating information.
Feature name
Rating entries
Hypothesis
If we provide rating entry points at contextually relevant moments, then we will increase rating submission volume because users can share feedback when they're naturally engaged and their experience is fresh.
View in prototype
Explore rating entry points across multiple touchpoints:
  • Cooking Flow: Rate during or after cooking experience
  • Past Orders: Rate completed meals from order history
  • Recipe Details: Rate from delivered recipe detail views
  • Push Notification: Timely reminders to rate meals
  • Loyalty Mission: Incentivized rating through rewards
Feature name
Cooking flow rating
Description
Integrated rating prompts within the cooking flow capture feedback at the moment of completion when the experience is fresh. This natural timing leads to more authentic, detailed ratings compared to delayed prompts.
View in prototype
You're viewing Step 6 of 6 - the final cooking step. Click the right arrow at the bottom to see how the rating flow integrates naturally at completion.
Feature name
Past orders rating entries
Description
The past orders section provides prominent rating entry points for completed meals, making it easy for users to rate multiple recipes in one session. This centralized approach increases rating volume by reducing the effort required to provide feedback.
View in prototype
Navigate to the past orders section to see rate buttons next to each completed order, with quick access to rating forms.
Feature name
Recipe details rating entries
Description
Rating entry points in delivered recipe detail views allow users to rate meals when they're actively reviewing recipe information. This contextual placement captures feedback from users who are already engaged with the recipe content.
View in prototype
Click into any recipe detail page from menu, cookbook, or past orders to find rating options integrated into the recipe view.
Feature name
Loyalty mission
Hypothesis
If we integrate rating activities into loyalty program missions with point rewards, then we will increase both rating volume and engagement because users are motivated by tangible rewards while the gamification makes rating feel more enjoyable and purposeful.
View in prototype
You're viewing the home page with the rating drawer automatically opened. Notice the purple mission card offering +400 points per rating and see the rating flow with loyalty mission and trophy coin animation.
Feature name
Push notification
Hypothesis
If we send timely push notifications prompting users to rate their delivered meals, then we will increase rating submission rates because the notification serves as a convenient reminder when the cooking experience is still fresh in their minds.
View in prototype
The prototype opens to show an iOS home screen. A push notification will appear at the top prompting you to rate your meal. Tap the notification to open the past orders page with the rating flow.
Feature name
Email rating entry
Description
Email prompts with embedded rating functionality provide a frictionless way for users to share feedback. By bringing the rating experience directly to their inbox, we remove barriers and capture ratings when users are already thinking about their meals.
View in prototype
Scroll through the email to see the mobile-optimized vertical layout. Each recipe card includes a mouth-watering photo, recipe name, and interactive star rating that allows users to rate directly from the email.
